Manchester city F.C.

About Man city

Manchester City Football Club is an English professional football club based in Manchester. The club was founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (West Gorton) and later became Ardwick Association Football Club in 1887 before finally adopting its current name, Manchester City, in 1894. The team has a rich history and has experienced both success and periods of struggle throughout its existence.

In recent years, Manchester City has emerged as one of the dominant forces in English and European football. Under the ownership of the Abu Dhabi United Group, led by Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the club has undergone significant investment, which has transformed it into a powerhouse in the sport.

Trophies won so far

English Premier League: Manchester City has won the Premier League title eight times. Their victories came in the seasons of 1936-37, 1967-68, 2011-12, 2013-14, 2017-18, 2018-19, 2020-21, and 2021-22. The club's recent successes under the management of Pep Guardiola have firmly established them as one of the dominant forces in English football.

League Cup/EFL Cup: Manchester City has had significant success in the League Cup, also known as the EFL Cup. They have won the trophy eight times, in the seasons of 1969-70, 1975-76, 2013-14, 2015-16, 2017-18, 2018-19, 2019-20, and 2020-21. The League Cup is a knockout competition exclusively for English clubs and provides an opportunity for teams to claim silverware during the season.
